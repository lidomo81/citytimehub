Irreplaceable Breaths: How Islam Transforms Time into Life

Published 8 July 2026 · 3 min read

Our faith inspires us with a wise vision that elevates respecting time to an act of worship, with the five daily prayers set at specific hours to teach us discipline. Allah Almighty swears by time in the Holy Quran to emphasize its gravity, as seen in the verse: "By time, indeed, mankind is in loss," warning us against wasting it. The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) further stresses personal accountability for every single moment, saying: "The feet of a slave will not move on the Day of Judgment until he is questioned about his life: how he spent it." From an Islamic perspective, true investment lies in wisely utilizing our health and free time before they slip away. Therefore, make every day a step forward, for time spent never returns.

Keep up with your prayer times, plan the month ahead with the monthly timetable, and start each day knowing exactly when the next prayer arrives.
