Why Do Prayer Times Change Every Day — and Differ Between Cities?

Have you noticed the call to prayer shift by a few minutes each day? Here's why.

Published 9 July 2026 · 5 min read

In this article you will learn:

  • Why prayer times change from one day to the next.
  • Why the Adhan is announced at different times in different cities.
  • Whether these daily changes are normal.
  • How prayer times are calculated with precision.

If you check your local prayer schedule regularly, you've probably noticed something interesting: today's Fajr prayer isn't at exactly the same time as yesterday's. Maghrib may come a little later, while Dhuhr and Asr also shift slightly throughout the year.

This often raises a simple question: if Muslims pray the same five daily prayers every day, why don't the prayer times stay the same? The answer has less to do with the clock on your wall and everything to do with the movement of the sun.

Prayer Times Are Based on the Sun — Not the Clock

Although we rely on clocks in our daily lives, Islamic prayer times were never tied to fixed numbers on a clock. Instead, each prayer begins when a specific natural sign appears:

  • Fajr begins with the appearance of true dawn.
  • Dhuhr starts when the sun passes its highest point in the sky.
  • Asr begins when shadows reach their prescribed length.
  • Maghrib starts the moment the sun sets.
  • Isha begins after twilight disappears.

Since these signs change slightly every day, the prayer times naturally change as well.

Why Do Prayer Times Shift Throughout the Year?

The Earth revolves around the Sun while rotating on a tilted axis. Because of this, the length of daylight and nighttime changes throughout the seasons.

During summer, daylight lasts longer, so sunset occurs later in the evening. In winter, the opposite happens: the days become shorter, the sun sets earlier, and the nights grow longer. As a result, the times for Fajr, Maghrib, and the other daily prayers gradually move forward or backward over the course of the year.

These daily adjustments aren't errors — they're a reflection of the precise natural order of our universe.

Why Do Prayer Times Differ Between Cities?

Even cities within the same country often have different prayer times. A city located farther east sees the sunrise before a city farther west. Since prayer times are determined by the sun's position, each city enters the prayer time according to its own location.

That's why Maghrib may already have begun in one city while another nearby city is still waiting a few more minutes for sunset. The greater the distance between two cities, the more noticeable the difference becomes — you can see it for yourself when you compare two cities and look at the time difference between them.

Can One Prayer Schedule Work Everywhere?

Not exactly. Accurate prayer times depend on several factors, including a city's geographical coordinates, the date, and the calculation method adopted in that region. For this reason, prayer times in Cairo are different from those in Alexandria, Riyadh, London, or Jakarta — even if your clock shows the same hour.

How Are Prayer Times Calculated Today?

In the past, people determined prayer times by observing the sun and the length of shadows. Today, modern applications and websites use precise astronomical calculations based on the sun's position and the exact coordinates of each city, so the times are worked out with great accuracy.

You may occasionally notice a difference of one or two minutes between different apps. In most cases, this is simply due to variations in calculation methods or local religious authorities — not because one of them is incorrect.

Why Is It Important to Check Prayer Times Daily?

Prayer is an act of worship that must be performed within its appointed time. As Allah says in the Qur'an:

"Indeed, prayer has been decreed upon the believers at specified times." (Qur'an 4:103)

Because prayer times change a little every day, it's important to rely on an accurate and up-to-date source — especially when travelling or living in a different city. You can follow the daily prayer times for your city, and view the full monthly timetable to plan ahead.

Final Thoughts

Those few minutes of daily change may seem insignificant, but they reflect the remarkable precision of the universe. Prayer times do not follow the hands of a clock; they follow the movement of the sun — a timeless system that has guided Muslims for centuries. This is why prayer times vary from one day to the next and from one city to another, while the purpose remains the same: to perform every prayer at its proper time.
