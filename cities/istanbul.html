00 — Overview

Time & prayer in Istanbul

Istanbul is the rare city that stands in two continents at once. The Bosphorus runs right through it, Europe on one bank and Asia on the other, and ferries cross between them all day long. It has been Byzantium, then Constantinople, then Istanbul — capital of two empires — and that long history still shows in its skyline of domes and minarets, from Hagia Sophia to the Blue Mosque. Its clock runs on Europe/Istanbul (UTC+3), which Turkey now keeps all year with no seasonal change. The time above is computed live in your browser, so it's right whether you're in the city or watching from elsewhere.

At around 41° north, Istanbul has a real swing between long summer evenings and short winter days, so its sunrise, sunset and prayer times shift noticeably from month to month, always following the sun.

Prayer times on this page follow Turkey's Diyanet (Presidency of Religious Affairs). Open the monthly timetable for the month ahead, the live prayer clock to watch the next prayer, or the Qibla tool to find the direction of Makkah from Istanbul.
