        How to Find the Best Time to Call Across Time Zones

        When the people you want to reach live in different time zones, the hard part isn't the time difference itself — it's finding the one window where everyone is awake and free at the same time. This tool flips the usual question around: instead of converting a single moment, it scans all 24 hours of your day and shows, for each city, whether that hour lands in an ideal time, a borderline time, or the middle of the night. The single best overlapping hour is surfaced at the top as your golden time.

        Pick a context to match what you're scheduling. Family call treats roughly 8am–11pm as a reasonable window to reach someone at home, while Work meeting narrows it to 9am–5pm office hours. Hours outside those ranges show as borderline, and late-night hours (about 11pm–7am) are flagged as asleep. Once you've found a good slot, the share link lets anyone open the same setup and read the result in their own cities.

        Frequently Asked Questions

        How does the best-time-to-call tool work?

        It converts every hour of your day into the local time of each city you add, then marks each as ideal, borderline or asleep based on the context you choose. The hour with the most people available is shown as the best time to call.

        What is the difference between the family and work contexts?

        The family context treats about 8am–11pm as acceptable for a personal call, while the work context uses standard 9am–5pm office hours. Switching between them re-scores every hour instantly.

        Does it handle daylight saving time?

        Yes. The conversions use each city's current UTC offset, so any daylight saving time in effect today is already reflected.

        Can I share the result?

        Yes. The share link encodes your city, the cities you added and the context, so anyone who opens it sees the same colour-coded overlap and best time.
