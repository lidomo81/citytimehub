      How the prayer clock keeps you connected to your family

      When you live far from home, the call to the five daily prayers is one of the things you miss most — that shared rhythm of the day. The prayer clock puts your city and your family's city side by side on two live clocks, each marked with the five prayers, so one glance tells you what they're up to right now: about to pray Maghrib while you're still at Dhuhr, or asleep before Fajr.

      Each clock shows the current local time with soft hands, with the five prayers in their real spots for that city. The next prayer is highlighted with a live countdown, and today's times are worked out from each city's exact location. The share link sends the same paired view to your family, so you're both looking at the same two clocks despite the distance.

      Frequently Asked Questions

      Where do the prayer times come from?

      They come from the AlAdhan calculation service using each city's exact coordinates and method, so the times match what your family sees locally.

      Does it update through the day?

      Yes. Both clocks run live, and the highlighted next prayer and its countdown refresh every second as the day moves on.

      Can I use any two cities?

      Yes. Pick any two of the cities — yours and your family's — and each clock loads its own prayer schedule.

      Can I share the view?

      Yes. The share link encodes both cities, so anyone who opens it sees the same two clocks side by side.
